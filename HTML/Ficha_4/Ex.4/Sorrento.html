Sorrento

  • Scenic Beauty: The coastline is characterized by steep cliffs, colorful houses clinging to the mountainside, and lush, terraced gardens. The views of the Mediterranean Sea are breathtaking.
    Historic Sites: Amalfi itself has a cathedral, the Cathedral of Saint Andrew, which dates back to the 9th century. The town was a powerful maritime republic in the past.
    Villages: There are several charming villages along the Amalfi Coast, including Positano, Ravello, and Praiano, each with its own unique character and attractions.
    Cuisine: The region is known for its delicious Mediterranean cuisine, featuring fresh seafood, locally grown fruits and vegetables, and traditional dishes.
    Lemon Groves: The Amalfi Coast is famous for its lemon groves, and limoncello, a lemon-flavored liqueur, is a popular local beverage.
    Hiking Trails: There are scenic hiking trails along the coastline, offering opportunities to explore the natural beauty of the area.
    Tourism: The Amalfi Coast is a popular destination for tourists, attracting visitors from around the world who come to enjoy its beauty, history, and culture
